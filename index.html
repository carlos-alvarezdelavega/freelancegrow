Freelance Grow: A Study about Potential Technologies for Online Freelancers

This research study explores online freelancers’ views of fictional technology concepts designed to improve the online freelancing work experience. In addition, we are interested in exploring how these fictional concepts might impact online freelancers’ work and identify directions for future technology and policy development. As part of the study, we will ask you to engage with our fictional concepts and participate in an online focus group to discuss your views of these technologies with other online freelancers.

This research project is funded and organised by Northumbria University (Ethics ID 35707). This study has no relation or association with any freelancing company or platform. If you have any questions, you can always reach out to us at: carlos.alvarez@northumbria.ac.uk

Who can participate?

We are recruiting professionals who use online freelancing platforms regularly to deliver freelance services, including but not limited to Upwork, Fiverr, Workana, Jovoto, FreeUp, Freelancer, and 99designs. We particularly welcome freelancers who have also used these platforms as clients.

To take part, you must fulfil the following requirements:

  • • Be 18 years or older.
  • • Have been an online freelancer (i.e., working independently through platforms) for at least three months.

Study stages


1. Recruitment Survey

In this survey, you will share some details about yourself and your online freelancing experience. This survey will determine if you are a suitable candidate for participating in this study. The survey takes between 5-10 minutes to complete. All this information will be kept confidential.

Take survey

2. Online Focus Group

We'll bring together up to five online freelancers to discuss the fictional technology concepts via Zoom. We're interested in exploring the group's perception of the fictional concepts, how they might be used in everyday work, and the implications for the work experience. The discussion will conclude with reflections on technology designers and policymakers' considerations when supporting the online freelancing work experience. The focus group will last up to two hours and accommodate various days of the week and times.

3. Follow-up Survey

One week after the focus group, you will receive a follow-up survey. This survey will ask for further thoughts you might have about our fictional technologies. In addition, this survey allows you to share additional reflections that were not discussed at the focus group.

Compensation

Upon attending the online focus group, you will receive an Amazon voucher worth £30 as compensation for your time.

FAQs


Do I have to take part?

No. Participation is entirely voluntary. If you take part, you are still free to withdraw at any time. You do not have to give a reason. If you withdraw, we will delete any data you would have provided.

How much time will I dedicate to the study?

The estimated time for each of the study stages is as follows:

  • • Recruitment survey: 5-10 minutes approx.
  • • Focus group: up to 2 hours.
  • • Follow-up survey: 15 minutes approx.

Will I be compensated for taking part?

Upon attending the online focus group, you will receive an Amazon voucher worth £30 as compensation for your time.

Will my taking part in this study be kept confidential and anonymous?

Yes. Your name will not be written on any of the data we collect; the written information you provide will have an ID number – not your name. Your name will not be written on the audio-recorded focus group, and your name will not appear in any reports or documents resulting from this study. All the data collected from you in this study will be confidential.

Download the participant information sheet for more details on what type of data we will collect and how we will protect it.
